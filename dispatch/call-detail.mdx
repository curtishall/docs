The **Call Detail** page is the complete, living record for a single Call for Service. Every piece of information tied to an incident — the location, assigned units, reporting parties, persons, vehicles, notes, and close details — lives here. You use call detail throughout the lifecycle of a CFS: from the moment a call is created through dispatch, scene management, and final closure.

## How to open a call

You can reach call detail from any of three entry points:

- **Dispatch board** — click any call card on an active board tab (Pending, Active, Recent, etc.).
- **After create** — Cadmus navigates to call detail automatically when you click **Create Call** in the New CFS form.
- **Find a Call** — use the [Find a Call](/dispatch/find-a-call) search for closed or historical calls that have scrolled off the board.

## App bar actions

The actions available at the top of call detail change based on the call's current status.

| Action             | When available                              |
|--------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| **Close Call**     | Active and open calls                       |
| **Reopen Call**    | Closed calls (subject to supervisor policy) |
| **Merge into…**    | Any open call — merge a duplicate CFS into this one |
| **Report Taken**   | When the agency's report workflow is active |

## Call detail blocks

Call detail is organized into labeled blocks. Each block focuses on a distinct aspect of the incident.

### Call summary

The call summary block shows the current **status**, **priority**, **incident type**, and most recent updates at a glance. From here you can:

- Click **Edit Call Details** to update priority, type, location, or narrative without reopening the New CFS form.
- View **RECENT UPDATES** for a condensed activity feed.

### Call type and description

This block displays the structured incident type code alongside the free-text **Call Description**. Use **Edit Incident Type** to change the type after initial create if information from the field warrants a reclassification.

### Call intelligence

The call intelligence block provides contextual information organized into three tabs:

| Tab          | Content                                                           |
|--------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Activity** | Prior incident activity associated with the current address.      |
| **Location** | Map and address details for the incident location.                |
| **Callers**  | Caller history and prior contacts associated with the RP number.  |

### Location details

The location block lets you manage address accuracy after the call is created:

- **Validate and Geocode Address** — re-submit the address to GIS and update the map pin.
- **Refresh Address Resolution** — pull the latest GIS result without changing the stored address.
- **Copy GPS coordinates** — copy the geocoded latitude/longitude to the clipboard for field communication.

<Warning>
  If the initial address was unvalidated, the map pin may be in the wrong location. Use **Validate and Geocode Address** as soon as you correct the address text.
</Warning>

### Units on call

The units block shows every unit currently assigned to the CFS and their real-time status.

| Status chip | Meaning                        |
|-------------|--------------------------------|
| **AVAIL**   | Unit is available               |
| **DISP**    | Unit dispatched to the call     |
| **ENRT**    | Unit en route                   |
| **SCENE**   | Unit on scene                   |
| **CLR**     | Unit cleared from the call      |

The **AVAILABLE UNITS** row at the top of the block lists units that can be immediately assigned. Click a unit in that row to dispatch it to the call without leaving call detail.

### Reporting party and callers

This block displays the primary **REPORTING PARTY** and any additional callers.

- Click **Query** next to a person record to run a CJIS person lookup.
- Click **Add caller** to record additional callers that contact the center after the call is created.

### Persons and vehicles

Use this block to document everyone involved in the incident, in addition to the reporting party.

**Person roles available:**

- Suspect
- Victim
- Witness
- Driver
- Passenger
- Other

For vehicles, click **Add Vehicle** to attach a vehicle record. Once a vehicle is added, click **Run CJIS vehicle query** to run a plate or VIN check against available databases.

### Assigned agencies

On multi-agency incidents, each involved agency receives its own CFS number and appears in this block.

- View each agency's CFS number alongside your own.
- Click **Generate report \#** to assign a report or case number for a specific agency.
- Click **Set Primary Agency** to designate the lead agency.
- Click **Remove Agency** to detach an agency from the call if added in error.

### Agency status timers

Each agency on a multi-agency call has its own timer card that tracks how long the call has been in the current status.

- Click **Status Check Complete** to acknowledge a timer check.
- Click **Pause checks** to temporarily suspend the timer when warranted.
- Use the interval and snooze options to adjust check frequency per agency SOP.

### History, logs, and images

This section is the call's permanent record of everything that happened.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Call Notes">
    Dispatcher-entered narrative notes attached to this call. Call notes are visible in the call record and may appear on reports depending on agency configuration. Click **Add Call Note** to add an entry.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Audit Logs">
    System-generated log of every change made to the call record — field edits, status updates, unit assignments, and more. Audit logs are read-only and cannot be deleted. Click **Add Log** to add a manual log entry for events not captured automatically.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Images">
    Photos and document attachments associated with the call. Click **Add Image** to paste or upload a file. Use **Take Photo** on devices with camera access.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Quick actions

The quick actions column provides one-click shortcuts to the most common dispatch tasks:

| Action                    | Effect                                                         |
|---------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Add Log Entry**         | Opens the add-log dialog for a manual narrative entry.         |
| **Assign Unit**           | Opens the unit search to assign a unit not in the available row. |
| **Assign Report Number**  | Associates a report or case number with this CFS.              |
| **Clear Call**            | Marks the call as cleared without a full close workflow.       |

## Closed calls

When a call's status is **Closed**:

- Editing most fields and adding new logs or images is restricted.
- The call remains visible in the **Recent** board tab for a configurable period, after which it moves to full history searchable via [Find a Call](/dispatch/find-a-call).
- Use **Reopen Call** only in accordance with your agency's supervisor-approval policy.

<Note>
  Reopening a closed call creates an audit log entry and may require a supervisor PIN or approval step depending on your agency's configuration.
</Note>
