Cadmus maps the most common dispatch actions to function keys so you can open dialogs, launch queries, and create pre-typed calls without lifting your hands from the keyboard. The F1–F5 keys are fixed across all agencies; F8–F12 are configured per organization and typically hold your agency's most-used quick call templates such as traffic stops, follow-ups, and paper service runs.

## Core Function Keys (F1–F5)

These five keys are available on every Cadmus dispatch console regardless of agency configuration:

| Key | Action | Description |
|---|---|---|
| <kbd>F1</kbd> | **New CFS** | Opens a blank Call for Service entry form |
| <kbd>F2</kbd> | **Login User** | Opens the Unit Assignment dialog to log crews on or off duty |
| <kbd>F3</kbd> | **Query** | Opens the NCIC/law-enforcement query panel (law-enforcement agencies with NCIC enabled) |
| <kbd>F4</kbd> | **Map** | Opens the dispatch map view |
| <kbd>F5</kbd> | **Units / Unit Greaseboard** | Opens the live unit status grid; supports table view, sort by status, and new-window mode |

<Note>
  <kbd>F4</kbd> (Map) may be associated with RapidSOS in some training or sandbox environments. In live production deployments, use the map-side tracking controls when the RapidSOS shortcut is not available.
</Note>

## Quick Call Templates (F8–F12)

Your agency administrator configures **Quick Call Shortcuts** in **Web Admin → Quick Call Shortcuts**. These shortcuts appear as labeled buttons in the Cadmus header and are also bound to function keys F8–F12. Common defaults include:

| Key | Typical Template |
|---|---|
| <kbd>F8</kbd> | **Traffic Stop** |
| <kbd>F9</kbd> | **Follow-up** |
| <kbd>F10</kbd> | **Paper Service** |
| <kbd>F11</kbd> | Additional org template |
| <kbd>F12</kbd> | Additional org template |

If your agency has more templates than there are function keys, the remaining templates appear in the **More Quick Calls** overflow menu in the header.

<Tip>
  Learn your agency's specific F8–F12 mappings early — they differ between organizations. Ask your CAD administrator or check **Web Admin → Quick Call Shortcuts** to see your current assignments.
</Tip>

## Traffic Stop Quick Call

The Traffic Stop quick call (typically <kbd>F8</kbd>) opens a focused workflow designed for officer-initiated traffic stops:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Traffic Stop">
    Press <kbd>F8</kbd> or click the **Traffic Stop** button in the header.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the plate">
    Type the license plate number in the plate entry field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run the plate (optional)">
    Click **Run Plate** to send a query before creating the call. The result populates vehicle and registered-owner information automatically when your agency has plate-query integration enabled.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the traffic stop">
    Click **Create Traffic Stop**. Cadmus creates a new CFS record using the traffic-stop call type template, pre-populated with the plate and any query results.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  The Traffic Stop quick call is only available when **Traffic Stops Allowed** is enabled in your agency's settings in Web Admin. If the button or function key does not appear, contact your CAD administrator to verify the agency configuration.
</Warning>

## Follow-Up Quick Call

The **Follow-up** quick call (typically <kbd>F9</kbd>) creates a CFS linked to prior activity:

- Use it when an officer needs to follow up on an existing incident without opening a brand-new, unrelated call.
- Depending on your agency configuration, the follow-up template may automatically assign the initiating unit when the call is created.

## Paper Service Quick Call

The **Paper Service** quick call (typically <kbd>F10</kbd>) uses a service-of-process template:

- Enter the service location and the unit performing the service.
- The resulting CFS record is typed as a paper-service run for accurate activity reporting.

## More Quick Calls Overflow Menu

If your agency has configured more than five quick call templates, the extras appear in the **More Quick Calls** menu in the header (next to the F8–F12 buttons). Click the menu to see all available templates.

## Tips and Best Practices

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Know your F-keys" icon="keyboard">
    Memorize your agency's F8–F12 layout. Quick calls are only fast if you do not have to search for the right button under pressure.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Reload after admin changes" icon="rotate">
    If your CAD administrator updates quick call templates, use **Reload Data** (or refresh the browser) to pick up the new assignments. Stale templates can point to outdated call types.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Verify location before submit" icon="location-dot">
    Quick calls create normal CFS records. Always confirm the incident location is accurate before submitting — the template does not auto-populate address from GPS unless your agency has that integration active.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Use F3 before F8" icon="magnifying-glass">
    For traffic stops, run the plate query with <kbd>F3</kbd> (or the in-template **Run Plate** button) before creating the call. Pre-populated vehicle data saves time and improves record accuracy.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>
  The RapidSOS header shortcut may be unavailable in some live deployments. If you rely on RapidSOS caller-location data, use the map-side RapidSOS tracking panel rather than the header shortcut.
</Warning>
